About Mexican Smalti

A living tradition

Mexican smalti has its roots in the pre-Columbian use of coloured stone and shell in decorative arts, evolving through Spanish colonial glassworking into the distinctive hand-poured glass tiles made in family workshops across Jalisco and Oaxaca today.

Each tile is individually poured, cooled, and cut — giving them the slight variation in thickness and the luminous, slightly rough surface that makes them so distinctive under light. Colours are mixed from mineral pigments, producing shades that won't fade.
